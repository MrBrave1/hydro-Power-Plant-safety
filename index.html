Emergency Devices
🌊
Flooding / Dam Breach / Structural Failure
Dam, Spillway, Drainage Galleries
CRITICAL
🛡️
Safety Devices 4 devices ▼
  • Water level sensors & ultrasonic depth gauges
  • Piezometers & inclinometers (structural movement)
  • Automated spillway crest gates
  • Inflow forecasting telemetry
✅
Preventive Measures 4 measures ▼
  • Conduct regular structural and bathymetric surveys
  • Maintain and test spillway gate mechanisms monthly
  • Implement upstream watershed weather monitoring systems
  • Keep drainage galleries free of debris and calcification
🚨
Emergency Actions 4 actions ▼
  • Sound plant-wide and downstream evacuation sirens
  • Initiate immediate controlled drawdown via spillway gates
  • Isolate powerhouse — drop intake gates & close main inlet valves (MIV)
  • Coordinate with local emergency disaster management authorities
🧰
Emergency Devices 5 devices ▼
  • Early warning sirens and public address systems
  • High-capacity emergency drainage pumps
  • Intake bulkhead drop gates (gravity-operated)
  • Emergency backup diesel generators (above flood levels)
  • Inflatable rescue boats and life rafts
⚡
Electrical Shock / Arc Flash / Electrocution
Switchgear, Busbars, High-Voltage Equipment
CRITICAL
🛡️
Safety Devices 4 devices ▼
  • Insulated busbar covers & barriers
  • Arc flash detection relays (light & pressure sensitive)
  • Safety interlock switches on switchgear enclosures
  • Grounding switches & permanent grounding pads
✅
Preventive Measures 4 measures ▼
  • Enforce strict Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) with double-verification
  • Mandate arc-rated clothing (Cat 2-4 PPE), voltage-rated gloves & face shields
  • Perform regular infrared thermography on transformers and switchgear
  • Maintain proper approach boundaries around high-voltage equipment
🚨
Emergency Actions 4 actions ▼
  • De-energize the affected sector immediately from remote control room
  • Extract victim using insulated rescue hook — never touch directly
  • Call emergency medical services; administer CPR/AED if trained
  • Secure the area to prevent secondary exposure
🧰
Emergency Devices 4 devices ▼
  • Insulated rescue hooks
  • Automated External Defibrillators (AEDs)
  • Emergency trip / isolation pushbuttons
  • Specialized burn first-aid kits
🕳️
Confined Space Entanglement & Atmospheric Toxicity
Penstocks, Scroll Cases, Inspection Galleries
HIGH
🛡️
Safety Devices 4 devices ▼
  • Multi-gas detectors (continuous monitoring)
  • Forced-air ventilation fans / blowers
  • Mechanical lockouts for upstream valves
  • Lockable entry hatches
✅
Preventive Measures 4 measures ▼
  • Implement strict Permit-to-Work (PTW) system for all entries
  • Perform pre-entry atmospheric testing: O₂, LEL, H₂S, CO
  • Deploy designated safety watch (standby person) at entrance
  • Establish continuous communication protocol (radio or tether)
🚨
Emergency Actions 4 actions ▼
  • Order immediate evacuation if gas levels spike or ventilation fails
  • Initiate retrieval plan using external mechanical winches
  • Administer emergency oxygen to rescued personnel
  • Do not allow standby watch to enter without proper breathing apparatus
🧰
Emergency Devices 4 devices ▼
  • Retrieval tripods with mechanical winches and harnesses
  • Self-Contained Breathing Apparatus (SCBA)
  • Emergency Escape Breathing Devices (EEBD — 15 min air)
  • Intrinsically safe (explosion-proof) flashlights and radios
⚙️
Mechanical Entanglement / Crushing
Turbine Shaft, Main Governor, Large Valves
CRITICAL
🛡️
Safety Devices 4 devices ▼
  • Heavy-duty fixed machine guards and enclosures
  • Safety interlock switches on access panels
  • Presence-sensing light curtains
  • Shaft rotation speed sensors
✅
Preventive Measures 4 measures ▼
  • Ensure all guards are securely bolted before machinery startup
  • Keep loose clothing, long hair, and jewelry completely secured
  • Establish clear visual and audible startup warning signals
  • Maintain clear, clean walking zones around rotating components
🚨
Emergency Actions 4 actions ▼
  • Depress the nearest Emergency Stop (E-stop) button immediately
  • Apply the mechanical turbine brake if safe and applicable
  • Secure machine using LOTO to prevent unexpected shifting during rescue
  • Provide immediate trauma first-aid for crush injuries or bleeding
🧰
Emergency Devices 4 devices ▼
  • Emergency Stop (E-stop) buttons (mushroom head)
  • Safety pull-cord switches along conveyors or long shafts
  • Hydraulic/pneumatic turbine brakes
  • Emergency trauma kits (tourniquets, heavy pressure bandages)
🔧
High-Pressure Hydraulic Failure / Oil Mist Injection
Governor Lines, Lubrication Systems
HIGH
🛡️
Safety Devices 4 devices ▼
  • Shielding guards and sleeves over high-pressure hoses
  • Pressure relief valves (PRVs)
  • Hydraulic accumulators with safety blocks
  • Oil mist detectors in enclosed spaces
✅
Preventive Measures 4 measures ▼
  • Conduct routine pressure testing and acoustic emission testing on lines
  • Enforce strict replacement schedules for flexible hydraulic hoses
  • Use non-destructive testing (NDT) to check for micro-cracks in steel piping
  • Never check leaks with bare hands — use cardboard or a mirror
🚨
Emergency Actions 4 actions ▼
  • Depressurize hydraulic system immediately via remote dump valves
  • Seek immediate specialized medical treatment for fluid injection injuries (time-critical)
  • Isolate electrical ignition sources if oil mist cloud forms
  • Contain spilled hydraulic oil using absorbents to prevent slips or waterway ingress
🧰
Emergency Devices 4 devices ▼
  • Emergency hydraulic dump valves
  • Spill containment kits (booms, absorbent pads, neutralizers)
  • High-pressure fluid injection warning tags
  • Fire-resistant barrier walls between hydraulic units
🔥
Fire & Explosion
Generator Stator, Transformers, Lubrication Oil Tanks
CRITICAL
🛡️
Safety Devices 4 devices ▼
  • Transformer blast walls (fire barriers)
  • Dissolved Gas Analysis (DGA) online monitors
  • Thermal imaging sensors and linear heat detection cables
  • Oil temperature indicators with alarm contacts
✅
Preventive Measures 4 measures ▼
  • Maintain transformer oil dielectric strength through regular filtering and testing
  • Keep vegetation and combustible materials cleared from switchyard
  • Ensure generator housing is free from oil leaks and carbon dust buildup
  • Regularly inspect and weight-test gas suppression cylinders
🚨
Emergency Actions 4 actions ▼
  • Trigger fixed fire suppression system manually if automatic sequence fails
  • Evacuate facility immediately via designated fire exit routes
  • Isolate electrical feeds to burning equipment to prevent tracking
  • Contain burning oil runoff within transformer catchment pit
🧰
Emergency Devices 4 devices ▼
  • High-pressure water deluge spray systems
  • CO₂ or clean-agent (Inergen/Novec) gaseous flooding systems
  • Fire-rated doors and dampers
  • Self-contained portable foam fire extinguishers
🪜
Slips, Trips, and Falls
Wet Areas, Open Gratings, Steep Catwalks
MEDIUM
🛡️
Safety Devices 4 devices ▼
  • Self-closing safety gates on ladders
  • Heavy-duty toe boards and robust handrails
  • Slip-resistant open steel floor grating
  • Safety netting under high work platforms
✅
Preventive Measures 4 measures ▼
  • Apply anti-slip coatings to high-traffic metal surfaces
  • Clean up oil leaks, grease, and water accumulation immediately
  • Enforce mandatory safety footwear with oil-resistant, high-grip soles
  • Implement 100% tie-off policy above 1.8 meters using appropriate anchors
🚨
Emergency Actions 4 actions ▼
  • Stabilize injured person — avoid moving if spinal injury is suspected
  • Deploy fall-arrest rescue team if worker is suspended in a harness
  • Clear the area to allow medical personnel unhindered access
  • Report structural failure or slip source immediately for barricading
🧰
Emergency Devices 4 devices ▼
  • Full-body safety harnesses and shock-absorbing lanyards
  • Fixed vertical ladder fall arrest systems (engineered cable/rail)
  • Suspension trauma straps (to prevent orthostatic intolerance)
  • Rescue stretchers (Spine boards / Basket stretchers)
🔊
Noise-Induced Hearing Loss & Acoustic Trauma
Generator Hall, Turbine Floor, High-Decibel Zones
MEDIUM
🛡️
Safety Devices 4 devices ▼
  • Acoustic enclosures around generators and turbines
  • Sound-absorbing wall insulation panels
  • Vibration isolation mounts under heavy machinery
  • Noise-level warning signs at entry doors
✅
Preventive Measures 4 measures ▼
  • Perform regular noise mapping of the entire plant floor
  • Enforce mandatory dual-protection policy (earplugs + earmuffs) in high-noise zones
  • Schedule regular worker rotation out of high-decibel areas to reduce daily dose
  • Conduct annual audiometric hearing exams for all operational personnel
🚨
Emergency Actions 4 actions ▼
  • Rotate worker out of high-noise area permanently if sudden acoustic trauma occurs
  • Provide immediate medical evaluation for sudden hearing loss or tinnitus
  • Adjust plant operations or dampen machinery if noise levels spike unexpectedly
  • Deploy portable noise barriers around temporary loud maintenance work
🧰
Emergency Devices 4 devices ▼
  • High-attenuation electronic noise-canceling earmuffs
  • Portable noise level meters / dosimeters
  • Custom-molded earplugs
  • Audiometric testing booths and equipment
